# POLLUTION MAN — PHASE 3 ATTACK PATTERNS

## Overview

Phase 3 combines specific attacks from Phase 1 and Phase 2 — two named attacks running simultaneously. The soul state is determined by whichever Phase 2 attack is in the combo. Bullets come from both outside and inside the box at the same time.

Each combo is a deliberate pairing where the two attacks create interactions that neither has alone. The Phase 1 attack pressures the player externally, the Phase 2 attack pressures them internally, and the overlap creates new problems and new safe zones that didn't exist in either attack individually.

Game-breaking UI attacks are layered on top of combos as modifiers, not standalone events.

## COMBO ATTACKS

---

### COMBO 1 — ACID RAIN + GRAVITY WELL

**Soul:** Blue

Acid Rain's concentric rings expand inward from above while Gravity Well's dense rainfall pours from the ceiling AND the floor rises from below. The player has increased gravity and reduced jump height.

The rings are coming down. The rain is coming down. The floor is coming up. The player is threading ring gaps with short jumps in a vertical space that's shrinking every second. By the end, they're hopping through ring gaps in a corridor barely three jump-heights tall with rain filling the gaps between rings.

**Why it works as a combo:** Acid Rain alone demands vertical freedom — push toward the rings, pick your gap. Gravity Well alone demands careful jump management in shrinking space. Together, the rings give structure to the chaos of the rainfall (the player focuses on ring gaps rather than individual raindrops), but the shrinking space makes each successive ring harder to thread. Each attack makes the other more dangerous AND more readable simultaneously.

---

### COMBO 2 — SMOG SPIRAL + TRACK SWITCH

**Soul:** Purple

Two spiral arms rotate outward from center while the four rail lines shift vertically in a wave pattern. The player is locked to the moving lines.

The spiral arms sweep across the lines as they rotate — but because the lines are moving up and down, the intersection points aren't predictable from the spiral alone. A line that was safe a second ago rises into a spiral arm's path. A line that was dangerous drops below it.

**Why it works as a combo:** Smog Spiral alone has a clean orbit strategy. Track Switch alone has a clean read-the-gaps strategy. Together, neither strategy works in isolation. The player has to track the spiral rotation AND the line movement and find moments where their current line dips away from the spiral arm's sweep. The moving lines create temporary safe windows that open and close rhythmically — the player surfs the wave.

---

### COMBO 3 — EXHAUST PIPES + MACHINE GUN ROULETTE

**Soul:** Green

Four sweeping streams descend from the top of the box (Exhaust Pipes, rotating back and forth) while two turrets rapid-fire from the box edges, relocating every 2.5 seconds (Machine Gun Roulette).

The player is locked in place with the snap shield. The sweeping streams from above are constant, predictable, and demand the shield face upward for long stretches. The turrets from the sides demand sudden lateral snaps. Every time the player snaps sideways to block a turret burst, the downward streams hit them. Every time they park the shield upward for the streams, the turrets hit them.

**Why it works as a combo:** Exhaust Pipes alone is a dodging pattern — move through the gaps in the sweeps. Under green soul, you CAN'T move. The sweeps become a constant directional threat you manage with the shield. Machine Gun Roulette alone is a reaction-snap challenge. Together, the player has to develop a rhythm: shield up during stream peaks, snap sideways during stream gaps to catch the turret burst, snap back up before the next stream peak. The turret relocations break the rhythm just when the player finds it.

---

### COMBO 4 — PIPELINE BURST + RISING SMOG

**Soul:** Blue

Three clusters fall from above and scatter after 1 second (Pipeline Burst) while horizontal bands of bullets rise from below with single gaps to jump through (Rising Smog).

The player is gravity-bound. They're jumping through Rising Smog gaps while Pipeline clusters are detonating above them — scattering bullets downward into the space they're jumping through. A jump that clears a Rising Smog band might launch the player into a Pipeline scatter zone.

**Why it works as a combo:** Rising Smog alone demands well-timed jumps at specific horizontal positions. Pipeline Burst alone demands reading cluster positions and getting clear before they pop. Together, the rising bands force the player to jump at specific moments, and the clusters punish being at the wrong HEIGHT during those jumps. The player has to time their Rising Smog gap-thread to happen BETWEEN Pipeline detonations — jump during the safe window, land before the scatter.

---

### COMBO 5 — TOXIC CLOUD BURST + ALTERNATING CURRENT

**Soul:** Purple

Radial bursts erupt from 3–4 points inside the box (Toxic Cloud Burst) while horizontal bullet streams run along all four rail lines, flipping direction every 2 seconds (Alternating Current).

The player is riding the stream gaps on their current line when a burst erupts — suddenly the line they're on has radial bullets cutting across it on top of the stream. They need to jump to a different line, but the other lines have their own streams AND might be closer to a different burst point.

**Why it works as a combo:** Alternating Current alone is a rhythm puzzle — ride the gaps, anticipate the flip. Toxic Cloud Burst alone is a positioning puzzle — get between the blast radiuses. Together, the bursts disrupt the stream-riding rhythm by forcing emergency line changes, and the streams restrict which lines are safe to jump to after a burst. The player can't just react to one system — they have to plan around both simultaneously.

---

### COMBO 6 — CHEMICAL RUNOFF + RING OF FIRE

**Soul:** Green

Crossing horizontal streams enter from both edges of the box (Chemical Runoff) while rings of bullets materialize around the player and fire inward, with 70% fakes and 30% real clustered in one direction (Ring of Fire).

The player is shield-locked. The crossing streams demand sustained sideways blocking. The rings demand reading the real cluster direction and snapping to it. When a ring fires during a stream wave, the player has to decide: stay facing the stream to block the heavy horizontal wave, or snap to the ring's real cluster direction and eat stream hits.

**Why it works as a combo:** Chemical Runoff alone is about finding overlap gaps — impossible when you can't move. Ring of Fire alone is about calm reading under pressure. Together, the streams create a BASELINE threat the player is constantly managing, and the rings are INTERRUPTS that force the player to break their stream-blocking rhythm. The skill is learning which interrupts are worth snapping for (dense real clusters close to the player) and which are better to tank (sparse clusters far away) while maintaining stream management.

---

### COMBO 7 — SMOG WALL + RAIL SHUTDOWN

**Soul:** Purple

Horizontal walls of bullets descend from above with single gaps (Smog Wall) while one of four rail lines shuts down every 2 seconds, cycling (Rail Shutdown).

The player is on rails. The descending walls have gaps they need to reach horizontally — but they also need to jump between lines to avoid the shutting-down rail. A wall gap might be directly above a rail that's about to go dead. The player has to thread the wall gap AND be on a safe rail when they do it.

**Why it works as a combo:** Smog Wall alone demands horizontal positioning for the gap. Rail Shutdown alone demands vertical positioning on a safe line. Together, each attack constrains a different axis. The player solves both simultaneously — "which line is safe AND can I reach the wall gap from that line's horizontal position?" Sometimes the answer is no, and the player has to take a wall hit to stay on a safe rail, or risk the dead rail to thread the gap. Forced trade-offs.

---

### COMBO 8 — AIMED BARRAGE + CONTAMINATION ZONES

**Soul:** Red

Pollution Man rapid-fires bullets directly at the player's position (Aimed Barrage) while 3–4 eruption zones pulse inside the box and detonate radially (Contamination Zones).

Free movement. The aimed shots force constant motion — stop moving, get hit. The contamination zones restrict WHERE you can move — certain areas are about to explode. The player is being herded by the aimed shots into paths that may run through a detonation zone.

**Why it works as a combo:** Aimed Barrage alone punishes stillness. Contamination Zones alone punish poor positioning. Together, the aimed barrage forces the player to keep moving, and the zones punish moving in the wrong direction. The player has to curve their movement path around zone positions while never stopping or reversing. It's like running a maze that's shooting at you.

## GAME-BREAKING MODIFIERS

*These overlay active combos. They don't replace attacks — they distort the conditions under which the attacks play out.*

---

### BREAK 1 — BOX CRUSH

Mid-combo, the box walls push inward over 4 seconds, compressing to roughly half width. The active combo continues in the smaller space. Same bullet count, half the room. Snaps back after 4 seconds.

---

### BREAK 2 — TEXT BOX ASSAULT

The dialogue box area begins spawning bullets upward into the combat box. Slow but persistent stream from below that removes the bottom third of the arena as safe space. Active combo continues above.

---

### BREAK 3 — INVERSION

The screen flips 180 degrees. Everything is upside down visually. Controls unchanged mechanically but the visual inversion scrambles the player's spatial processing. Active combo continues inverted. Lasts 6 seconds.

---

### BREAK 4 — SOUL SCRAMBLE

Once per Phase 3. No warning flash. Soul color changes mid-combo instantly. Whatever the worst possible transition is for the current situation. Only happens once — it's an ambush, not a mechanic.

---

### BREAK 5 — ERROR FLOOD

Fake Windows 95 error pop-ups stack on top of the combat box, partially obscuring visibility. Active combo continues behind the visual clutter. Errors fade after 5 seconds. Most effective when overlaid on green soul combos where the player needs to see bullet directions.

## FINAL ATTACK — EVERYTHING HE HAS

Triggers at roughly 5% HP after the Phase 3 dialogue sequence.

**Structure:** 32 seconds. Soul changes every 8 seconds: Red → Blue → Purple → Green. All transitions telegraphed normally. No game-breaking tricks. No UI manipulation. Clean arena.

**Red (0–8s):** Contamination Zones + Smog Spiral. Radial bursts inside the box layered with rotating spiral arms from outside. Free movement through overlapping patterns.

**Blue (8–16s):** Acid Rain + Geyser. Expanding rings from above, erupting columns from below. Jump through ring gaps while hopping over geysers. The sandwich at maximum density.

**Purple (16–24s):** Line Sweep + Alternating Current. Vertical bars sweep across the lines while directional streams flip. Jump lines to dodge sweeps, ride gaps in the streams.

**Green (24–32s):** Chemical Runoff + Cardinal Shots. Crossing streams from both edges while sequential and then simultaneous directional bullets demand rapid shield snaps. Fastest reads in the fight.

After 32 seconds, attacks stop. Pollution Man stands still. The player's auto-fire is still active. The last few hits land automatically. Health bar empties.

## CYCLING RULES — PHASE 3

- Opens with COMBO 1 (Acid Rain + Gravity Well) to immediately signal the fusion.
- Combos randomize after that, no immediate repeats.
- Game-breaking modifiers insert between combos — one break every 2–3 combos, overlaying the active combo.
- SOUL SCRAMBLE happens exactly once. Save it for when the player has found a rhythm.
- ERROR FLOOD should overlay a green soul combo at least once.
- Below 15% HP, combos chain with zero downtime.
- At ~5% HP, current combo ends and the Final Attack triggers after the dialogue sequence.

## TUNING NOTES

- Combos should occasionally create accidental safe zones where the two patterns' threat areas don't overlap. These breathing moments are essential — constant pressure without relief is exhausting, not fun.
- Game-breaking modifiers are rare. The player might see 3–4 across the entire phase. They should feel like events.
- The Final Attack is hard but honest. No tricks. Every pattern is familiar. Every transition is telegraphed. The difficulty is endurance and adaptation across all four soul states at high density. The fairness is the point.
- The 3+ second pause after the Final Attack before auto-fire finishes Pollution Man is non-negotiable. The player needs to sit in that silence and realize the game is killing him, not them.
